## Logo

### Terminology
- **Symbol:** the Sophos shield and emblem
- **Wordmark:** the Sophos logotype
- **Logo:** the full lockup with symbol and wordmark used together

### Corporate Logo
Shield icon + "SOPHOS" wordmark. Always used together in communications.

### Logo with Tagline
"DEFEAT CYBERATTACKS" below the wordmark.

### Logo Lockups
- SOPHOS | CENTRAL
- SOPHOS | AI
- SOPHOS | X-OPS
- SOPHOS | PARTNERS

### Clear Area
Use the height and width of the "O" in the logo to maintain minimum clearance between the logo and other elements on all sides.

### Usage Rules
- The logo must be used as provided and cannot be distorted
- Don't add shadows, outlines, bevels, or gradients
- Avoid placing over busy or high-contrast backgrounds
- Do not isolate the symbol, wordmark, or logo as a design feature

### Approved Background Combinations
- **Dark Blue** (`#001A47`) background → white logo ✓
- **Sophos Blue** (`#2006F7`) background → white logo ✓
- **Gradient** (Cyber Blue → Sophos Blue) → white logo ✓
- **White** background → Dark Blue logo ✓
- Logo + tagline versions approved on same backgrounds

### Shield Symbol
- Should NOT replace the full logo in communications
- May be used in isolation under different circumstances
- Contact brand@sophos.com for approval

### Wordmark Only
The SOPHOS wordmark (no shield) may only be used when a primary logo is already in proximity.

## Tone

We use direct and concise language, avoiding industry jargon and superfluous detail. We write for an international audience. Style defaults to U.S. spellings globally; U.K. spellings may be used in regional communications. Based on AP Style.

- **Confident and bold:** Inspire trust, highlight expertise, communicate complex cybersecurity solutions with clarity and confidence. Showcase expertise with confidence, but not arrogance.
- **Clear and approachable:** Avoid technical jargon unless required. Explain sophisticated ideas in ways anyone can understand.
- **Active and outcome-driven:** Tie the message back to customer benefits and business results: safety, trust, focus, and growth.
- **Duality-focused:** Balance cutting-edge technology and expert-driven guidance. Use contrasts to highlight the synergy of Sophos technology and expertise working together.

## Style Guide

### Abbreviations
- Define abbreviations the first time (e.g., "Active Directory (AD)")
- Common abbreviations (USB, UTM, OS) don't need defining
- No periods in abbreviations (IT, not I.T.)
- Exception: country abbreviations (U.K. and U.S.)

### Acronyms
- Acronyms pronounced as words → lowercase (e.g., laser, sonar, scuba)
- Technical acronyms → capitals (CD-ROM, WYSIWYG)
- Pluralize without apostrophe (PDFs, CD-ROMs)
- Articles: use "a" or "an" based on pronunciation ("an FTP server," "a UTM")

### Apostrophes
- Show possession: the customer's complaint, the companies' future
- Never for ordinary plurals (not "apple's")
- Its = belonging to it; It's = it is/it has; Its' is never right

### Bold
- Use sparingly to emphasize words, numbers, and dates
- Avoid bold italic (hard to read)

### Bullet Points
- Period if a complete thought; no period for individual items

### Capitalization
- Only capitalize proper names, product names, headlines, and sentence beginnings

### Commas
- Use Oxford comma (serial comma before and/or)

### Contractions
- Use contractions (it's, isn't) unless writing formally

### Dates
- Format: Sept. 15, 2010 (abbreviate months longer than five characters)
- Decades: the 1990s or '90s (never 1990's)
- Regional formats acceptable for regional communications

### Ellipses
- Always space before and after: ( ... )

### Em Dash (—)
- Space before and after
- May replace commas, semicolons, colons, parentheses

### Graphs
- Use numerals (1, 2, 3) in graphs

### Headlines
- Complete sentences → Sentence case with punctuation
- Incomplete sentences → Title Case without punctuation

### Hyphens
- Use for compound words (full-disk encryption)

### Italics
- Avoid; they make writing look cluttered

### Money
- Symbol without space: US$123.45, CDN$123.45, €1,234.56

### Names
- First mention: full name. Then last name only.

### Numbers
- Words for one through nine; numerals for 10+
- Measurements always use numerals (5 cm, 70 mph)
- Large numbers: comma separated (1,234,567)
- Whole numbers don't need .00

### Parentheses
- Use parentheses (like this), not brackets [like this]
- Full sentence inside → period inside. Partial → period outside.

### Percentages
- Write "percent" in text (e.g., 16 percent growth)
- "%" acceptable in graphs and charts

### Phone Numbers
- U.S.: +1-866-866-2802
- International: +44 (0) 1235 559 933

### Places
- Initial capitals: Boston, Paris, New York

### Compass Directions
- Lowercase, no hyphens (north, southeast, northwest)
- Capitalize established regions (the South, Pacific Northwest, Southeast Asia)

### Quotations
- Double quotation marks with comma introduction
- Punctuation goes inside quotes
- Single quotes for a quote within a quote

### Sophos
- **Always singular.** "Sophos is trusted by 100 million users."
- Never say "Sophos are."

### Than vs. Then
- Than = comparison; Then = sequential time/instructions

### Times
- a.m. and p.m. (not AM/PM)
- 3 p.m. not 3:00 p.m.
- 12 noon or 12 midnight
- Default to 12-hour clock and American date structure for worldwide copy

### Trademarks
- Reference Sophos-owned trademarks in first instance only, not after

### Underline
- Don't underline words for emphasis

### URLs
- No need for http:// prefix
- In body copy, preface with www
- No initial capitals in web addresses (sophos.com not Sophos.com)

### We
- Use "we" to sound personal and team-like
- Clarify context: is it your team, your business, or the whole company?

### Your vs. You're
- Your = belonging to reader; You're = you are
